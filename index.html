How to Form Teams (The Tricky Part!)

With 50 players, dividing into teams is the first game itself! Here are creative ways to randomly (and chaotically) group everyone up before the balloon game:

1. Puzzle Pieces

Hand out cut-up pieces of different pictures as people arrive. Players must find the others with the matching pieces to complete the picture and form their team.

2. Colored Candies

Have everyone draw a candy (like Skittles or M&Ms) from an opaque bag. Your team is everyone who pulled the same color.

Blanket Name Game

Icebreaker · Fast Reflexes · Quick Memory

How to Play

  • 1 Split the group into two large halves.
  • 2 The organizers hold up a big black carpet or blanket between the two groups so they can't see each other.
  • 3 Each group silently selects 1 or 2 players to sit directly in front of the blanket.
  • 4 On the count of three, the organizers drop the blanket! The selected players must race to spit out the name of the opponent(s) facing them. The first to say the correct name wins the round!

Balloon Team Scatter

Teamwork · Chaos · Coordination

How to Play

  • 1 After forming teams, give every player a balloon and a permanent marker.
  • 2 Players write their names clearly on their balloons.
  • 3 All players toss their balloons into the center of the room, and organizers scatter them wildly.
  • 4 On "GO", players run into the center. They must find the balloons belonging to their specific teammates, gather them, and sit down in a circle as a completed team. First team to reunite with all correct balloons wins!
