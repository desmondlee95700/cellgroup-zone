# Frontend Design Anti-Patterns

Use this reference when the brief is open-ended or when the first design idea feels too familiar.

## Common AI Defaults

These patterns are sometimes valid, but they should be choices, not reflexes:

- Warm cream background with high-contrast serif display type and terracotta accents.
- Near-black background with one acid accent color and vague futuristic copy.
- Broadsheet layout with hairline rules, dense columns, and zero radius regardless of subject.
- Oversized gradient hero, floating translucent cards, pill badges, bokeh blobs, and generic stats.
- Three cards under a hero where the content is not naturally a set of three.
- Decorative "01 / 02 / 03" markers when order has no real meaning.
- Vague screenshots or abstract mockups where a real product, place, state, or interaction would be more informative.
- All sections wrapped in cards, producing a page of boxes rather than a coherent layout.

## Replacement Moves

When a design falls into a default, ask what object, place, rule, or behavior belongs to the subject.

- For a game, lead with play state: score, timer, teams, tokens, board, motion, or physical setup.
- For an operational tool, lead with the work surface: filters, queue, table, status, comparisons, and quick actions.
- For a venue, person, brand, or product, show the actual thing in the first viewport.
- For a technical product, expose the workflow or artifact the user manipulates instead of hiding behind abstract claims.
- For a community or event, use rituals, signage, schedules, group dynamics, and local language as visual material.

## Palette Checks

After writing CSS, scan the dominant colors. If the page reads as mostly purple/purple-blue gradients, beige/cream/tan, dark slate, or brown/orange without a brief-specific reason, revise the palette.

Each accent should have a job. If two colors perform the same role, remove one or separate their meanings.

## Decoration Checks

Remove any visual element that cannot answer at least one of these:

- What content does it clarify?
- What state does it reveal?
- What action does it make easier?
- What subject-specific cue does it carry?
- What rhythm or hierarchy does it improve?
